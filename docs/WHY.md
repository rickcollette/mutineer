# Mutineer. A Bulletin Board System

## What's the dealio, yo?

Ok - so in 1981 I caught the online bug. Bad. I needed to run my own BBS.  I hacked up a super buggy version of A.M.I.S. BBS for the atari 800, and started my little bulleting board.  It was on an 800 with stock 48K RAM, a 300 baud modem I had to build an auto answer device for,  and a 90K floppy disk.  That was it.

Over the years I swapped BBS software and platforms - going from AMIS to FOReM XEP, to a slung together system that was written in KSH and some support utilities for MINIX, then eventually Linux, BSD, Linux again. Then I stopped.

About 10 years ago I started the idea of porting the concept of Renegade BBS to C, a language I could work in way more freely than Pascal - which I was complete ass at coding in.

This kept going on and off again over the decade.  Now, I am at it again, and taking what I had completed and morphing it into something that would have been most excellent in the late 80's or early 90's and is about as impressive as a bottle of nothing today.

Go me.
